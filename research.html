Phyllodulcin and Alzheimer's

A 2023 study found that phyllodulcin, a natural sweetener from hydrangea flowers, reduces amyloid-β aggregation and improves memory in Alzheimer’s model mice. Behavioral and brain tests showed improved hippocampal function, reduced plaques, and decreased neuroinflammation, suggesting phyllodulcin may be a promising, lower-cost candidate for future Alzheimer’s treatments.

Published in Biomedicine & Pharmacotherapy, 2023.
